Affliction Warlock DPS

Affliction Warlocks change from a support role in The Burning Crusade over to high throughput DPS in Wrath of the Lich King. The gameplay gets a significant overhaul for the better, no longer does Affliction essentially use the same rotation with perhaps one damage-over-time ability extra. The biggest reason for this is the significant changes that happen to Corruption and Drain Soul.

The initial application of Corruption can potentially keep going on forever thanks to Everlasting Affliction. This talent keeps on refreshing your Corruption when you cast either Shadow Bolt, Haunt, or Drain Soul. But, that isn't everything it does. Due to the nature of the refresh and Corruption being a damage-over-time ability some niche mechanics get introduced. Certain effects get recalculated when Corruption is refreshed through Everlasting Affliction, whilst others stay active based on the initial application and do not get applied when you get them after Corruption is already up. Spells that keep being active even after the refresh is called effects that "roll over". The result of all this is that we want to get an as strong as possible initial application of Corruption and keep it up as long as possible.

Drain Soul only saw use for grinding out Soul Shard Soul Shard in the past. Those days are over, Drain Soul now is major high damage dealing execute ability. Once the target reaches quarter health the damage increases by fourfold. This results in it instantly becoming the highest damage per second dealing ability (DoTs still deal more damage for the time it takes to apply them, hence you want to keep certain ones active still during the execute phase). Just like Corruption has some special mechanics due to being a damage-over-time ability, the same happens to Drain Soul because it is a channeled spell. Certain effects will snapshot and apply to the full duration of Drain Soul upon application (and not apply if procced when already casting Drain Soul, which could warrant casting the spell again to get them to apply). Besides that being a thing, you also want to keep certain DoTs up based on how long the fight will still last. This means you will have to cancel ( called "clipping") your Drain Soul, reapply your DoT and recast Drain Soul, all whilst keeping track of what snapshots.

Demonology Warlock DPS

Demonology Warlocks become more viable and sought after than any of the previous expansions. The biggest reason for this is Demonic Pact, this talent causes your Felguard to become a buff god. Our spell power is always in flux due to cooldowns and procs. What this means for Demonic Pact is that the amount of spell power it provides also is variable. The way it works is that stronger versions (aka that provide more spell power can overwrite weaker ones after a 5-second internal cooldown. The last 10 seconds of the buff weaker versions also can overwrite stronger ones, this decreases the odds of Demonic Pact falling off.

We don't only provide a lot of spell power via Demonic Pact. We also have great burst damage and cleave DPS. Releasing our inner demon with Metamorphosis we become Illidan's little brother. Whilst in demon form we deal 20% more damage plus we unlock a couple of unique abilities which can further our damage. Immolation Aura and Shadow Cleave significantly increase our DPS if we are cleaving enemies within the melee range. A Demonology Warlock that knows when to use and how to use Metamorphosis will stand out from the rest.

Being the cool Warlock specialization we also like to switch it up when it comes to our filler spells. Shadow Bolt is the main filler spell that we use, but whenever Molten Core procs from Corruption we will be spamming Incinerate. And once our targets are about to die we get out the big guns with Soul Fire.

Destruction Warlock DPS

Destruction has always been key and center when it comes to raiding for Warlocks in previous expansions. Come Wrath of the Lich King more options open up with Affliction and Demonology taking a step forward and both becoming great options each with their niche. However, that doesn't mean that Destruction isn't a viable option anymore. If anything Destruction has seen some reworks which finally fully support being a Fire Warlock like how we have been dreaming about ever since we learned Searing Pain. Incinerate becomes our main filler spell, along with Conflagrate and Chaos Bolt that we will use on cooldown mixed with keeping Immolate up at all times. Raining down fel fire on all who stand to oppose us.

We get a lot more to play around with instead of only casting Shadow Bolt. The biggest overhaul is seen to Conflagrate which finally sees use inside player versus environment. Fire and Brimstone causes Conflagrate to crit more often then not. Glyph of Conflagrate Glyph of Conflagrate in turn keeps us from having to recast Immolate every 10 seconds. Another major selling point is Backdraft, the increase in casting speed not only a DPS increase but also feels great.

Improved Soul Leech is another big boon for Destruction Warlocks, and with it we finally bring support to the raid.
